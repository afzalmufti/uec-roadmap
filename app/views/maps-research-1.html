MAP RESEARCH

Found examples of interesting and sometimes successful uses of datasets + geography. Usually with a map, but not always.

Luminocity uses the 2011 Census data to create a fascinating interactive map tool. The hexagonal tiles/stacks are good, as are the heatmap palettes.

Fun. But, this is kind of what we're after - an overview of the best area for a specific industry.

Poorly executed but this map does pack a lot of info into a single image.

LEFT: Hex grid again, but note the joined up tiles to create regions.

RIGHT: Hex grid used to apply population density as a distortion to the natural map. Getting away from 100% geographic seems useful.

Three variations of the rail map as simplified and abstracted to provide maximum information on connectivity without being too bothered about absolute geographic accuracy.

Three variations mapping datapoints onto the map, highlight that it clearly has limitations with even a relatively low number of points.

Showing the abstraction of geography while making the data better understood - the linear plot does keep the geographic groupings of countries, which seems useful.
